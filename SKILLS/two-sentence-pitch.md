# Skill: two-sentence-pitch

Write the concise "what is it + why it's interesting" description anyone can understand.

## The two jobs
1. **What is it?** — so plain that someone with zero context knows what the thing is.
2. **Why is it interesting/valuable?** — why it's noteworthy vs. the alternatives.

## Procedure
1. Strip jargon. If a smart outsider couldn't repeat what it is, rewrite sentence one.
2. For sentence two, name what makes it different or non-obvious — not adjectives, a reason.
3. Test: read it cold. Do you know what it is AND why you'd care? If not, iterate.

## Output
Two sentences. Used in decks, updates, intros, and your own chief-of-staff positioning.
